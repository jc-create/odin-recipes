Almond Flour Breaded Chicken Breast

Recipe Description

Chicken breast recipes have always alluded me. Chicken breast tends to dry out very easily, and will usually taste bland without some sort of panko breading or marinade. Breading the meat with seasoned almond flour has been a "hack" for a healthier, low carb, gluten free alternative to traditional panko breading.

This is another very quick recipe where the only preparation involves butterflying/flattening breasts. Here, we will be roasting the breasts, however, feel free to pan fry them in a skillet with some oil!

Almond flour does not adhere to the breast as well as breadcrumbs, so try keeping one hand dry to sprinkle flour, and the other hand to handle the breast itself during the breading steps. Also be sure to let excess egg drip off the breasts before breading, as this will help with the crispiness of the chicken.

Unfortunately the cooked breasts do not stay that crispy if reheated in a microwave, rather than an oven

Allergies Warning: contains nuts/almonds

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon italian seasoning/basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • cooking oil spray

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 425F degrees. Line baking sheet with foil and and wire rack, spray sheet with oil
  2. Butterfly/Pound chicken breast to desired thickness, the more even, the better. Pat dry.
  3. In one bowl, whisk eggs. In another bowl, mix almond flour, garlic, paprika, onion powder, italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  4. Dip breast into eggwash, allow excess egg to drip off. Coat in the flour mixture. Tip: keep one hand dry to sprinkle flour on top, so the other hand can handle the breast.
  5. Set breaded breasts on baking tray/wire rack and spray the tops and bottom with oil. bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  6. Best served immediately,
